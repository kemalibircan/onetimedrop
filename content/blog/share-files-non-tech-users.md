## The Challenge: Helping Non-Tech Users Receive Files

Technical users often underestimate how confusing file sharing can be for others. "Just send it on WhatsApp" — but what if they can't find the file? "Upload to Google Drive" — but they don't have an account.

The ideal method for non-technical users is:
- Involves as few steps as possible
- Doesn't require creating accounts
- Has minimal typing
- Gives them a clear success signal

## The Best Methods for Non-Tech Users

### Method 1: QR Code (Zero Typing Required)

The absolute simplest method for in-person sharing (same room):

1. You open [onetimedrop.io](/) on their computer
2. A QR code appears on screen
3. They scan it with their phone camera — the join page opens automatically
4. You tell them: "Select the file from your Files or Photos app and tap upload"
5. They download it on the computer

The only tech they need to know: how to open their camera app.

### Method 2: Email With a Direct Download Link

For remote non-tech users, email is still reliable:
- Send the file as an attachment (under 25MB)
- They click the attachment in their inbox — it downloads automatically

Simple, familiar for most people, no new apps to learn. The downside: quality, size limits, and inbox permanence.

### Method 3: WhatsApp (If They're Already On It)

If the person uses WhatsApp regularly, sending a photo or file there requires zero new learning on their part. The trade-off is file compression and privacy, but for low-stakes everyday sharing, it works.

### Method 4: Narrate Every Step

When helping a non-tech user with OneTimeDrop:

1. "Open the website onetimedrop.io on the computer" (or do it for them)
2. "Now on your phone, open your camera and point it at this square on the screen"
3. "Tap the notification that appears"
4. "Do you see a button that says 'Select files'? Tap that"
5. "Pick the photo you want to send"
6. "Now look at the computer screen — it should show your photo there"

Verbal, step-by-step guidance eliminates confusion.

## What Doesn't Work With Non-Tech Users

- Anything involving creating accounts
- Methods requiring drivers or app installs
- Services with too many options/settings
- Bluetooth pairing (confusing error messages)

## Which Format Is Easiest for Non-Tech Users to Open?

For photos: **JPEG** — universally understood, opens in any Photo viewer.  
For documents: **PDF** — "just click it, it opens."  
Avoid HEIC, DOCX, PPTX if the person doesn't have Office or a Mac.
