## Phone Scanning Has Gotten Very Good

Modern phones can scan documents to PDF in seconds using apps like:
- **iOS built-in:** Notes app → Camera → Scan Documents
- **Android:** Google Drive app → Scan
- **Third-party:** Adobe Scan, Microsoft Lens

The problem isn't scanning — it's getting that scan off your phone and onto a computer quickly.

## The Fastest Transfer Method: QR Session

Once you've scanned your document, use [OneTimeDrop](/) to move it to the computer:

### Step 1: Scan your document

On iPhone (built-in):
1. Open the Notes app
2. Create a new note → tap the camera icon
3. Select "Scan Documents"
4. Photograph your document
5. Tap "Save" — the scan saves as a PDF attachment in the note
6. Tap the PDF → Share → Save to Files

On Android (Google Drive):
1. Open Google Drive → blue + button
2. Select "Scan"
3. Take the photo → crop if needed
4. Save — it goes to Google Drive (then download to your phone's storage)

### Step 2: Transfer to the computer

1. On the computer, open [onetimedrop.io](/)
2. On your phone, scan the QR code (or visit [onetimedrop.io/join](/join))
3. Tap **Select files** → find your scan in Files/Downloads
4. Upload → click Download on the computer

Total time from scan to computer: under 2 minutes.

## Scan Quality: What Settings to Use

| App | Quality | Output format | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| iOS Notes | Excellent | PDF | Auto-perspective correction |
| Google Drive Scan | Excellent | PDF | OCR-indexed in Drive |
| Adobe Scan | Excellent | PDF | Best for multi-page docs |
| Camera (raw photo) | Variable | JPEG | No automatic cleanup |

For printing or archiving, always use PDF output. For photos of receipts/screenshots, JPEG is fine.

## Privacy & Safety Tip

> ⚠️ **Disclaimer:** OneTimeDrop files are automatically deleted after 10 minutes. If you're scanning sensitive documents (ID cards, contracts, medical forms), ensure you're on a private and trusted computer. Always delete the downloaded file from public computers after use.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I export a scan as PDF on iOS?**  
A: Scan in Notes → tap the scan → tap Share → choose "Save to Files" to save as PDF. Then select it in OneTimeDrop.

**Q: Can I scan multiple pages into one PDF?**  
A: Yes. In the iOS Notes scanner, keep tapping the shutter button for each page. The final save creates a multi-page PDF.

**Q: What if my scan is over 50MB?**  
A: Scan compression is extremely efficient. A 10-page document is typically 1–5MB. If it's larger, reduce the scan resolution in your app settings.
