## Why Video Transfer Goes Wrong

Large videos expose every weak transfer method. Email rejects them, messaging apps shrink them, and cloud sync may take longer than you expect. If the clip matters for editing, work, or delivery, you need the original file on the computer.

That means direct transfer beats convenience sharing.

## Best Workflow for Most Cases

1. Keep the original video in Photos or Files on your phone.
2. Open [OneTimeDrop](/) on the destination PC.
3. Scan the QR code and upload the file directly.
4. Wait for the transfer to complete before switching apps or locking the phone.
5. Compare file size on the PC to confirm you received the right version.

## When to Use Another Method

### Use a Cable
Best for very large batches or unstable Wi-Fi.

### Use Cloud Storage
Best if the editor is remote or multiple people need access later.

### Do Not Use Messaging Apps
Most chat apps are designed for convenience, not source-quality delivery.

## Comparison

| Method | Quality | Speed | Drawback |
|---|---|---|---|
| Direct browser transfer | Original | Fast | Needs active session |
| USB cable | Original | Fast | Hardware friction |
| Cloud upload | Original | Slower | Sync time |
| Messaging app | Often reduced | Fast to send | Quality loss |

## FAQ

**Q: How do I know if quality was reduced?**  
A: Check file size, resolution, and bitrate on the computer against the original.

**Q: Can I send 4K videos this way?**  
A: Yes, as long as the service limit and your network can handle it.

**Q: Should I zip the video first?**  
A: Usually no. Most videos are already compressed, so zipping helps very little.
