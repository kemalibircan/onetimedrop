## First Question: What Is Actually Broken?

A broken phone can mean many things. If the screen is damaged but touch still works, you still have several good options. If the port is dead but the phone turns on and joins Wi-Fi, wireless transfer may save you. If the phone does not boot at all, your options narrow fast.

So start by separating display damage from power or storage failure.

## Best-Case Recovery Workflow

If the phone still unlocks and connects to Wi-Fi:

1. Open a browser or Files/Photos access point on the phone.
2. Open [OneTimeDrop](/) on a nearby computer.
3. Scan the QR code if the camera still works, or enter the session code manually.
4. Transfer the most important files first: IDs, tickets, contracts, recent photos, scans.
5. Back them up immediately after download.

## If the Screen Barely Works

Prioritize only the files you need today. A damaged device may fail completely later, so urgent triage matters more than perfect organization.

## When Wi-Fi Will Not Help

Wi-Fi transfer will not solve a dead battery, failed storage, or a phone that cannot unlock. In those cases you may need a repair shop, a display replacement, or a vendor-specific recovery path.

| Situation | Can Wi-Fi help? | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Cracked screen, phone still usable | Yes | You can still start a transfer |
| Charging port damaged | Often yes | Wireless path avoids the port |
| Phone will not power on | No | Storage is inaccessible |

## FAQ

**Q: Should I transfer everything at once?**  
A: No. Get the irreplaceable files first while the phone is still usable.

**Q: What if the camera is broken too?**  
A: Use the manual session code instead of QR scanning if the device can still open the join page.

**Q: Is cloud sync better here?**  
A: Only if it was already configured before the damage. Setting it up on a failing device may be too slow.
