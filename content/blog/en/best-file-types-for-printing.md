## The Format Matters for Printing

You've sent the file. You're at the computer. You hit Print. And the result looks wrong — text is blurry, layout is shifted, images are squeezed. The problem? Wrong file format.

Here's what to use, and when.

## PDF: Almost Always the Best Choice

**PDF (Portable Document Format)** was literally invented for printing. It locks in your fonts, layout, images, and margins. What you see is exactly what prints.

✅ Use for:
- Any document with text (letters, CVs, reports, forms)
- Presentations (exported from PowerPoint/Keynote)
- Scanned documents
- Tickets and boarding passes
- Invoices

**How to create PDF on iPhone:**
Any app (Word, Notes, Pages) → Share → Print → Pinch to expand preview → Share again → Save as PDF

**How to create PDF on Android:**
Google Docs/Office → Share → Print → Save as PDF (select from printer list)

## JPEG: Good for Photos, Not for Text

**JPEG (.jpg)** is excellent for photographs but terrible for anything with crisp text or line art, because:
- JPEG uses lossy compression — images get slightly blurry
- Text and sharp lines look "muddy" at JPEG quality settings
- Each re-save degrades quality further

✅ Use for:
- Printing photographs
- Photo books
- Wall prints

❌ Avoid for:
- Documents with text
- Logos or diagrams
- Presentations

## PNG: Great for Transparency, Overkill for Photos

**PNG** uses lossless compression — quality is preserved perfectly. It's great for screenshots, logos, and anything with transparency layers. But PNG files are larger than JPEG, which can slow transfer slightly.

✅ Use for:
- Screenshots
- Graphics with transparent backgrounds
- Logos, icons

## HEIC: iPhone's Format — Needs Conversion

**HEIC** is Apple's space-efficient photo format. It's great for storage, but not universally supported:
- Windows may not open HEIC without the HEIC codec extension
- Older printers and software don't support it

**Fix:** Before printing, convert HEIC to JPEG:
- iPhone: Settings → Camera → Formats → Most Compatible (forces JPEG capture)
- Or use a free converter app

## DOCX / PPTX: Only If the Computer Has Microsoft Office

Word and PowerPoint files will print fine IF the computer has Office installed. If not:
- The file may open in LibreOffice with layout issues
- Some printers won't see these formats at all

**Safer choice:** Export to PDF before sending.

## Quick Reference

| Format | Text | Photos | Diagrams | Universal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **PDF** | ✅ Perfect | ✅ Good | ✅ Perfect | ✅ Yes |
| **JPEG** | ❌ Blurry | ✅ Excellent | ❌ Poor | ✅ Yes |
| **PNG** | ✅ Sharp | ✅ Good | ✅ Sharp | ✅ Yes |
| **HEIC** | ✅ Sharp | ✅ Excellent | ✅ Sharp | ❌ Partial |
| **DOCX** | ✅ Good | ✅ Good | ✅ Good | ❌ Needs Office |

## FAQ

**Q: I have a PNG but need to print a giant poster — will it look OK?**  
A: PNG is lossless so quality won't degrade. However, make sure the image resolution (DPI) is high enough for the print size.

**Q: Can I convert a Word doc to PDF on my phone before transferring?**  
A: Yes. Microsoft Word Mobile and Google Docs both offer PDF export.

**Q: Does OneTimeDrop convert file types?**  
A: No. OneTimeDrop transfers files in their original format without modification.
