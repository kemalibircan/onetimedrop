## The Quality Problem

WhatsApp is great for fast communication, but it is a weak archive. Images may already be compressed by the time they reach the chat, and exported media quickly becomes mixed with screenshots, forwarded copies, and duplicate saves.

If you need the best version available on your phone, move the file out of chat and transfer it directly.

## Best Workflow

1. Save the media from WhatsApp into Photos or Files first.
2. Open [OneTimeDrop](/) on your computer.
3. Scan the QR code with your phone.
4. Upload the saved file from Photos or Files, not by forwarding it through another chat app.
5. Download it immediately and check the file size on the computer.

This keeps the transfer path simple and avoids another round of compression.

## Important Detail

If the file was already compressed inside WhatsApp, no transfer method can restore lost quality. The goal is to preserve the best copy you still have.

## Comparison

| Method | Quality outcome | Main issue |
|---|---|---|
| Save then direct transfer | Best available copy | Manual step |
| Forward to another chat | Often worse | More compression |
| Email from phone | Usually okay for docs | Extra inbox clutter |
| Cloud sync | Good | Slower for urgent handoff |

## FAQ

**Q: Does WhatsApp document mode help?**  
A: Yes. If the sender used document mode instead of image mode, the received file is often closer to the original.

**Q: How do I verify quality?**  
A: Compare resolution and file size on the computer against what you see on the phone.

**Q: Can I transfer whole chat exports this way?**  
A: Yes, if the export creates files available in your phone storage.
