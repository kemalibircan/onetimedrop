## Why This Moment Matters

Portfolio sharing is often a trust moment. You do not want to fumble with logins, ask for app installs, or scroll through your entire camera roll in front of a client. The best portfolio handoff is clean, selective, and fast.

That is why a temporary transfer beats opening your whole cloud storage account on someone else's computer.

## Recommended Client Workflow

1. Curate the files before the meeting starts.
2. Open [OneTimeDrop](/) on the client's computer or the presentation laptop.
3. Scan the QR code from your phone.
4. Upload the small set of portfolio assets you want to show.
5. Download and present from the larger screen.

## Why It Works

You stay focused on the work, not on setup. There is no need to reveal unrelated files, old drafts, or personal albums while searching for the right images.

## Good Formats for Portfolio Handoff

| Format | Best for | Note |
|---|---|---|
| PDF | Decks and case studies | Most predictable on unknown computers |
| JPG / PNG | Visual samples | Good for quick fullscreen review |
| MP4 | Motion work | Test playback controls first |

## FAQ

**Q: Is this better than sending a website link?**  
A: If your portfolio site is perfect and online, a link is fine. A direct file handoff is better when you need specific offline assets or a custom deck.

**Q: Should I use a shared folder?**  
A: Not for a one-time client conversation. It creates unnecessary exposure and clutter.

**Q: What if the client wants the files afterward?**  
A: Send a follow-up link or approved package later. Keep the meeting transfer focused on presentation.
