## AirDrop Only Works Between Apple Devices

AirDrop is Apple's wireless file sharing feature — and it's great, until you need to send photos to a Windows PC, a work computer, or a school computer you've never paired with. In those cases, AirDrop is simply not an option.

Here's an easy alternative that works anywhere.

## The Universal Solution: Browser QR Transfer

[OneTimeDrop](/) is a browser-based tool that works on iPhone, Windows, Mac, Android — you name it. You don't install anything:

1. On the **computer**, open [onetimedrop.io](/)
2. A large QR code and 8-digit code appear
3. On your **iPhone**, scan the QR with the Camera app (or open [onetimedrop.io/join](/join))
4. Tap the code, then the join page opens
5. Select your photos and upload

Photos appear on the computer in full original quality — no compression, no iCloud sign-in.

## Sending iPhone Photos: What You Need to Know

### HEIC vs. JPEG
iPhones often save photos in HEIC format, which Windows may not support natively. OneTimeDrop transfers the file as-is. If you need JPEG format, go to **Settings → Camera → Formats → Most Compatible** on your iPhone before taking photos.

### Live Photos
Live Photos will transfer as `.HEIC` (still) + `.MOV` (video). Both files transfer, but the animation only works in Apple's Photos app.

### iCloud syncing is not required
OneTimeDrop works directly from your Camera Roll, not through iCloud.

## Step-by-Step: iPhone Photos to Windows PC

1. On the **Windows PC**, open Chrome/Edge and go to [onetimedrop.io](/)
2. The session code and QR appear immediately
3. On your **iPhone**, open the Camera app and scan the QR
4. Tap the notification banner to open the join link
5. The phone connects — tap **Select files**
6. Choose **Photos** — select one or multiple
7. Tap Upload — watch progress on screen
8. On the PC, click **Download** next to each photo

Total time: under 30 seconds for a single photo.

## Privacy & Safety Tip

> ⚠️ **Disclaimer:** Files are automatically deleted from OneTimeDrop's servers after 10 minutes. Never upload private or sensitive photos on public or shared computers.
