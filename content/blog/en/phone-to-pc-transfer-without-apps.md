## The Problem With Traditional File Transfer Methods

If you've ever tried to send a file from your phone to a computer that isn't yours, you know the struggle. USB cables require drivers. Bluetooth is slow and finicky. AirDrop only works between Apple devices. WhatsApp compresses images. Email has attachment limits — and leaves copies everywhere.

There's a better way, and it requires nothing but a browser.

## The Quickest Method: Browser-Based QR Transfer

Services like [OneTimeDrop](/) let you transfer files using a session code and QR code. Here's why this method wins:

- **No installation required** on either device
- **Works on any browser** — Chrome, Safari, Firefox, Edge
- **Cross-platform** — iPhone to Windows, Android to Mac
- **Files auto-delete** after 10 minutes for privacy

## Step-by-Step: Transferring a File in Under 30 Seconds

1. **Open a tab on the computer** — Go to [onetimedrop.io](/)
2. **A QR code and 8-digit code appear** — No sign-up, instant
3. **On your phone, scan the QR** — Or visit [onetimedrop.io/join](/join) and type the code
4. **Pick your files** — Tap "Select files" on your phone
5. **Download on the computer** — Files appear instantly; click Download

That's it. No cables, no accounts, no compression.

## Privacy & Safety Tip

> ⚠️ **Disclaimer:** Files on OneTimeDrop are stored temporarily and automatically deleted after 10 minutes. Do not upload sensitive personal documents if you are on a shared or public computer.

When using any browser-based transfer service, always check that:
- The URL begins with `https://`
- You are on a trusted network (not a coffee shop open Wi-Fi if possible)
- You close the session or clear files when done
