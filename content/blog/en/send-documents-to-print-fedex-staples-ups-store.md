## The Goal

When you are sending documents to a print shop, the real goal is not just transfer. You need the file to arrive in the right format, open correctly on their computer, and stay as private as the situation allows.

That means preparation matters as much as the transfer method.

## Best Workflow

1. Convert the document to PDF if possible.
2. Double-check page size, margins, and signatures on your phone.
3. Open [OneTimeDrop](/) on the print-shop computer or on a staff-approved device.
4. Transfer only the final file you want printed.
5. Confirm the preview before paying, then clear the file from the machine if you controlled the session.

## Why PDF Wins

A PDF reduces font issues and layout surprises. Word documents and presentation files can shift if the print computer does not have the same fonts or software versions.

## Where People Lose Time

- Showing up with multiple versions of the same file
- Sending screenshots instead of the original document
- Relying on a chat app that compressed the file
- Forgetting to check page size before printing

| Method | Best for | Strength | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| PDF via direct browser transfer | Fast counter service | Cleanest workflow | Requires brief access to a device |
| Email to print desk | Remote prep | Familiar | Inbox delay and confusion |
| USB drive | Offline backup | Good fallback | Easy to leave behind |

## FAQ

**Q: Should I use JPG instead of PDF?**  
A: Only for image-based prints. For documents, PDF is usually the safer format.

**Q: Is it okay to print signed forms this way?**  
A: Yes, but treat the print computer as shared equipment and clean up carefully.

**Q: What if the staff asks for email only?**  
A: Send the PDF, but keep the filename clear and remove unnecessary pages first.
