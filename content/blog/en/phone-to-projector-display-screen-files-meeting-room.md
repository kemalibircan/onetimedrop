## The Problem in Real Life

Meeting rooms fail at the worst time: the HDMI cable is missing, the presentation laptop is not yours, or the projector only talks to an old Windows machine in the room. In those moments, the fastest fix is often not mirroring your entire phone screen. It is getting the right file onto the room computer quickly.

That is why screen display and file transfer should be treated as two separate decisions.

## Fastest Reliable Workflow

1. Open the room computer and make sure the projector is already connected.
2. Open [OneTimeDrop](/) on that computer.
3. Scan the QR code from your phone and upload the presentation, PDF, or video.
4. Download it locally and open it with the software already on the room machine.
5. Use screen mirroring only if you truly need live app interaction.

This is usually more stable than trying to force wireless casting on an unfamiliar setup.

## When Mirroring Makes Sense

### Use Screen Mirroring
When you need to demo an app, show gestures, or move through content that only exists on your phone.

### Use File Transfer Instead
When you are presenting slides, a PDF agenda, product photos, or a short video. Local playback from the room computer is more predictable and less likely to stutter.

## Comparison

| Approach | Best for | Strength | Weakness |
|---|---|---|---|
| Transfer the file first | Slides, PDFs, videos | Stable, easy control | Requires download step |
| Wireless mirroring | Live app demo | Real-time screen view | Can lag or fail on unknown hardware |
| Cable from phone | Controlled setup | Direct signal | Adapter compatibility issues |

> Tip: always carry the file in PDF as a fallback even if the main deck is in PowerPoint or Keynote.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the best format for projectors?**  
A: PDF is the safest universal fallback. PPTX is fine if you trust the room computer and fonts.

**Q: Can I present directly from my phone browser?**  
A: Sometimes, but the controls are usually worse and notifications can interrupt. A room computer is cleaner.

**Q: What if there is no internet?**  
A: Then cable-based transfer or a USB drive may be your fallback. If Wi-Fi exists locally, browser transfer is still often enough.
