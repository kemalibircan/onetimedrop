## Why iPhone to Windows Still Needs a Plan

iPhone and Windows can work together well, but the path depends on what you are moving. Photos, short videos, PDFs, and scanned documents are easy. Full-device backups, app data, and very large media libraries take more planning.

If your goal is just to get files onto a Windows computer quickly, avoid the heavyweight setup unless you really need it.

## Best Method for Most People

1. Open [OneTimeDrop](/) on the Windows PC.
2. Scan the QR code with your iPhone.
3. Choose the files from Photos or Files.
4. Upload and download them on the PC.
5. Rename or sort them once they land in Downloads.

This is the simplest path for ad hoc transfers and does not depend on AirDrop or iTunes.

## Best Method by File Type

### Photos
Use browser transfer if you need selected images right now. Use iCloud Photos only if you already rely on it for backup and sync.

### PDFs and Documents
Files app plus browser transfer is usually fastest because the file stays in its original format.

### Large Video Clips
Transfer directly instead of sending through chat. Messaging apps often reduce quality and make later editing harder.

## Method Comparison

| Method | Best for | Strength | Weakness |
|---|---|---|---|
| Browser transfer | One-off files | Fast, no desktop setup | Manual each time |
| iCloud | Ongoing sync | Works in Apple ecosystem | Slower for urgent handoff |
| USB import | Large batches | Strong control | More steps and trust prompts |
| Email/chat | Tiny files | Familiar | Compression and size limits |

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need iTunes for this?**  
A: Not for ordinary file handoff. You only need desktop management tools for broader sync or backups.

**Q: Can I move files from the Files app and not just Photos?**  
A: Yes. That is one of the strongest parts of the browser workflow.

**Q: Is this different from AirDrop?**  
A: Yes. AirDrop is Apple-only. A browser session works with a Windows PC too.
