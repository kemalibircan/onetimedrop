## Why People Want to Skip the Cloud

Cloud accounts are fine for long-term storage, but they are often the wrong tool for immediate transfer. You sign in, upload, wait for sync, open another app, find the file again, then clean up later. That is a lot of steps for a single PDF or a few photos.

If your goal is simple handoff, the fastest method is usually live browser pairing.

## The Shortest Workflow

1. Open [OneTimeDrop](/) on the receiving computer.
2. Scan the QR code with the sending phone.
3. Upload the file.
4. Download it on the computer.
5. Close or clear the session when done.

That is faster than creating a shared folder, especially on a borrowed or temporary machine.

## Why It Beats Cloud Accounts for Speed

| Method | Steps involved | Typical slowdown |
|---|---|---|
| Browser pairing | Open, scan, send, download | Minimal |
| Cloud account | Sign in, upload, sync, open, download | Sync and account friction |
| Email | Attach, send, receive, download | Attachment limits and inbox delay |

## When Cloud Still Wins

Cloud storage is still the better option when you need version history, teamwork, or a permanent archive. It is just not the fastest choice for same-room or same-hour transfers.

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need both devices on the same network?**  
A: It is usually easiest, but the main point is that both devices can reach the transfer service at the same time.

**Q: Is this better than messaging the file to yourself?**  
A: Yes for quality, organization, and speed. Messaging adds another app and often compresses media.

**Q: What if I need to move ten files?**  
A: Batch upload works well for small sets. For a full archive, a dedicated sync workflow is better.
