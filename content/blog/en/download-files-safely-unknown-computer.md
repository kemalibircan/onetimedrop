## What Makes a Computer "Unknown"?

An unknown computer is any computer that isn't yours and isn't fully under your control:
- Hotel business center
- Library workstation
- Copy shop terminal
- Friend's or family member's PC
- Office computer from a different department

The risks of using these computers involve both what you leave behind (files, browser history) and what the computer might have running on it (malware, keyloggers).

## Step 1: Assess the Risk Level

**Low risk:** Friend's personal laptop. They're nearby, you trust them, it's temporary.

**Medium risk:** Office computer managed by IT. Clean, but company IT may have monitoring software.

**High risk:** Library or hotel computer. Multiple users, unknown software, potential keyloggers. Minimise what you do here.

## Step 2: Use Private/Incognito Mode

Before opening any browser tab to transfer files, switch to Incognito/Private mode:
- **Chrome:** Ctrl+Shift+N (Cmd+Shift+N on Mac)
- **Firefox:** Ctrl+Shift+P
- **Safari:** Cmd+Shift+N

This prevents the computer from saving browser history, cookies, passwords, or autofill data from your session.

## Step 4: Use the File, Then Delete It

After downloading and using your file (printing, opening, etc.):

1. **Delete from Downloads folder** — Shift+Delete (Windows) or right-click → Move to Trash (Mac)
2. If you emptied the trash, the file is gone from the file system (though not forensically unrecoverable — but fine for typical use)
3. Close all browser tabs

## Step 5: Sign Out and Clear Any Sessions

- Close the OneTimeDrop tab — your session auto-expires in 10 minutes anyway
- If you accidentally logged into any website, log out and revoke sessions from your phone

## Privacy & Safety Tip

> ⚠️ **Disclaimer:** OneTimeDrop auto-deletes files from the server after 10 minutes. However, files you download remain on the computer's hard drive until manually deleted. On public computers, always delete the file immediately after use.

## What If the File Is Sensitive?

If the file contains sensitive information (legal document, medical record):
- Choose medium-risk or better computers only
- Use mobile data, not unknown Wi-Fi
- Delete after use, then empty the Recycle Bin/Trash

## FAQ

**Q: Can the IT department see files I transfer on their computer?**  
A: Potentially yes — IT can monitor network traffic and installed agents may log file activity. Use your own device for sensitive transfers.

**Q: Does Incognito mode protect me from keyloggers?**  
A: No. Incognito only prevents browser history/session storage. Keyloggers operate at the OS level.

**Q: What is the safest way to move files on an unknown computer?**  
A: Use a trusted network, private browsing, transfer what you need, delete immediately after.
