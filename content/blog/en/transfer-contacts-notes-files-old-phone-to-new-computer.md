## Why This Is Different From Normal File Sharing

Moving data from an old phone to a new computer is not just about one file. It is a mini migration. The risk is forgetting where each type of data lives: contacts in one app, notes in another, files in local storage, and photos somewhere else entirely.

Start by separating what needs export from what already syncs automatically.

## A Clean Migration Order

1. Export or sync contacts using the platform's official method.
2. Export notes if they are not already in a synced account.
3. Gather local files, PDFs, and media you actually want to keep.
4. Use [OneTimeDrop](/) or another direct transfer method for the files that must move now.
5. Organize everything into folders on the new computer immediately after transfer.

## What Usually Needs Manual Attention

- Downloaded PDFs and forms
- Voice notes and miscellaneous app exports
- Notes stored locally instead of in a cloud account
- Older photos you do not want to leave on the device

## Comparison

| Data type | Best move |
|---|---|
| Contacts | Official export or account sync |
| Notes | App export or PDF export |
| Local files | Direct browser transfer |
| Photos | Selective transfer or full archive workflow |

## FAQ

**Q: Should I move everything or only selected files?**  
A: Move the important local files first. Full cleanup can happen later.

**Q: Can I do this without trusting a cloud service?**  
A: Yes for many file types. Direct transfer works well for local documents and media.

**Q: What is the biggest mistake?**  
A: Assuming all important data already syncs somewhere automatically.
