## Why a Tablet Can Be the Better Scanner

A tablet gives you a larger view for framing pages, checking corners, and reviewing scans before you send them. For receipts, signed forms, worksheets, or multi-page notes, that can be more comfortable than scanning on a phone.

The only mistake is scanning well and then getting stuck on transfer.

## Simple Workflow

1. Use the built-in scanner in your tablet's Notes, Files, or camera app.
2. Save the result as a PDF if the app allows it.
3. Open [OneTimeDrop](/) on your computer.
4. Scan the QR code from the tablet or use the join code.
5. Upload the PDF and download it on the computer.

## Why PDF Is Best Here

Scanned documents are easier to organize and print when they arrive as a single PDF instead of a loose set of images.

## Comparison

| Output | Best for | Strength | Weakness |
|---|---|---|---|
| PDF scan | Documents | Easy to print and share | Less flexible for image editing |
| JPG pages | Photos or receipts | Simple image access | Harder to manage in batches |

## FAQ

**Q: Can I scan multiple pages?**  
A: Yes. Most tablet scanning apps support multi-page capture into one PDF.

**Q: Is a tablet scan good enough for official use?**  
A: Often yes for informal workflows, but always confirm the recipient's requirements.

**Q: Why not just email the scan?**  
A: You can, but direct transfer is faster and avoids inbox clutter when the computer is right there.
