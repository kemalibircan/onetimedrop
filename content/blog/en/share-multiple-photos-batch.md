## Why Batch Photo Transfer Is Tricky

Sending one photo by email is easy. Sending 15 high-res photos is a different story:
- Email caps out at 25MB — a batch of HEIC photos gets there fast
- WhatsApp compresses each image (and takes forever to queue 20 photos)
- Google Photos sharing requires the recipient to have an account
- AirDrop only works on Apple and needs proximity

Here's how to do it properly.

## Batch Transfer With OneTimeDrop

[OneTimeDrop](/) supports selecting and uploading multiple files at once, up to 20 files per session (50MB each).

### Step-by-Step: Sending 20 Photos

1. On the computer, open [onetimedrop.io](/) — your code and QR appear
2. On your phone, scan the QR (or go to [onetimedrop.io/join](/join))
3. Tap **Select files**
4. In the photo picker, tap and hold on the first photo, then tap each additional one (on iOS/Android, multi-select works with the native picker)
5. Upload all files — progress bar shows per file
6. On the computer, each file has its own Download button
7. Or click **Download all (ZIP)** to get them in one archive

### Tip: iPhone Multi-Select in the Files Picker

When OneTimeDrop opens the iPhone file picker, tap **Browse** → **Photos** → tap **Select** in the top right → tap each photo. All selected photos upload together.

### Tip: Android Multi-Select

In the Android file picker from a browser, long-press the first photo, then tap additional ones to select. They'll all upload in one go.

## How to Organize Before Transfer

If you're sending a large batch, consider creating a folder on your phone first:

1. On iPhone: Files app → iCloud/On My Phone → Create folder → move files in
2. On Android: Files by Google app → Create folder → move photos in

This helps you keep track of what you've uploaded.

## When 20 Files Isn't Enough

OneTimeDrop supports 20 files per session. If you need more:
1. Transfer the first 20 and download them
2. Click "Clear session" on the desktop
3. Upload the next batch on your phone

Or, zip files on your phone first to reduce the number of items (e.g., 50 photos → 1 zip file).

> ⚠️ **Disclaimer:** OneTimeDrop files auto-delete after 10 minutes. If you're downloading a large batch, start the downloads promptly after upload.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I upload a zip file of photos?**  
A: Yes! If you zip photos on your phone first, a single large zip file counts as one upload.

**Q: Does uploading 20 photos at once slow things down?**  
A: Uploads happen sequentially in the browser — one after another. Each photo takes 1–5 seconds depending on size.

**Q: Will photo EXIF data (location, timestamp) transfer intact?**  
A: Yes. OneTimeDrop transfers files as-is, including all metadata.
