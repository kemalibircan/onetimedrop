## Why WhatsApp and Email Are Bad at File Transfer

WhatsApp automatically compresses photos and videos, often reducing quality significantly. Email limits attachments to 25MB (sometimes less), and copies of your files can sit in inboxes indefinitely.

If you're sharing files you care about — high-resolution photos, original PDFs, work documents — you need a better tool.

## 5 Better Options for File Sharing

### 1. Browser-Based Session Transfer (Best for One-Time Transfers)

Services like [OneTimeDrop](/) let you create a temporary session. Scan a QR code or enter a code, upload your file, download it on the recipient's end — done. Files auto-delete after 10 minutes. No account needed.

**Best for:** Phone-to-computer transfers, printing at copy shops, one-time private sharing

### 2. AirDrop (Apple Devices Only)

Apple's AirDrop works over local Wi-Fi and Bluetooth. Files arrive in full quality. But it only works between Apple devices on the same network — no Android, no Windows.

**Best for:** iPhone to Mac, if both are nearby and Apple

### 3. Snapdrop / Local.Errichto

Snapdrop is an open-source project that enables local network file transfer via WebRTC. Great for sharing between devices on the same Wi-Fi, no setup required.

**Best for:** Sharing between devices on the same home or office network

### 4. WeTransfer Free Tier

WeTransfer lets you send up to 2GB without an account. Recipients get a download link by email. Files expire after 7 days.

**Best for:** Sending large files to someone remotely (by email link)

### 5. Shared Cloud Drive (Quick Share Link)

Google Drive, iCloud, or Dropbox all support "share a link" features. Right-click any file and generate a link. The recipient doesn't need an account to download.

**Best for:** Files you want to share repeatedly or over a longer period

## Privacy & Safety Tip

> ⚠️ **Disclaimer:** OneTimeDrop files are deleted after 10 minutes. Always verify the tool you use stores data responsibly. Avoid uploading sensitive files (IDs, contracts) on public computers regardless of the tool.

## FAQ

**Q: Which method is most private?**  
A: OneTimeDrop and AirDrop are most private. Files never end up on someone's email server or chat app.

**Q: What if I need to share with someone far away?**  
A: WeTransfer or a cloud drive link works well for remote sharing.

**Q: Can I use OneTimeDrop internationally?**  
A: Yes — both devices just need internet access.
