## Why PDF Transfer Is Different

PDFs are often important: contracts, boarding passes, invoices, presentations. You don't want them compressed. You don't want them saved in email servers. And you want them on the right computer right now.

Here's how to do it properly.

## The Best Method: Browser Transfer (Fastest, Most Private)

[OneTimeDrop](/) is purpose-built for exactly this use case:

1. Open [onetimedrop.io](/) on your laptop — code and QR code appear instantly
2. Scan the QR with your phone (or visit [onetimedrop.io/join](/join) and type the code)
3. On your phone, tap **Select files** — choose your PDF
4. Upload completes in seconds (typical PDF: 1-5MB)
5. Click Download on the laptop

The PDF arrives exactly as it left your phone. No compression, no account sign-in.

## Step-by-Step for Common Scenarios

### Boarding Pass PDF
1. Find the PDF in your email or downloads
2. Follow the OneTimeDrop steps above
3. Open the downloaded PDF in Adobe Reader or browser to confirm it looks correct

### Work Contract or Legal Document
1. Use OneTimeDrop on a trusted private network only
2. After downloading, immediately use "Clear session" to delete files from the server
3. Never transfer legal documents on public library computers

### Lecture Slides (PPTX → PDF)
1. Convert on your phone first: in PowerPoint Mobile, use Export → PDF
2. Transfer via OneTimeDrop
3. Open on the laptop

## Privacy & Safety Tip

> ⚠️ **Disclaimer:** Files on OneTimeDrop are auto-deleted after 10 minutes. If you're transferring sensitive PDFs (medical, legal, financial), make sure you're on a secure network and use a private/incognito browser window on shared computers. Delete the file from Downloads after use.

## Pro Tip: Verify the PDF After Download

Always open the downloaded PDF and scroll through it quickly to ensure all pages transferred correctly before closing the session.
