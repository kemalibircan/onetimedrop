## The Pre-Meeting Panic

You're in the conference room. Your slides are on your phone but the presentation needs to go onto the room's laptop — and that laptop is locked down: no Bluetooth, no shared drive access, and you can't log into the room's account.

Sound familiar? Here's what to do in under two minutes.

## The 2-Minute Pre-Meeting Solution

### Step 1: QR Session on The Room's Laptop

1. Open a browser on the conference room laptop (Chrome/Edge/Safari)
2. Go to [onetimedrop.io](/)
3. The code and QR appear — keep the window visible

### Step 2: Connect From Your Phone

4. Scan the QR with your phone camera (or [join by code](/join))
5. Select your presentation file — PPTX, PDF, or Keynote export

### Step 3: Download and Present

6. Download the file on the laptop
7. Open directly and start presenting

Total time: 90 seconds if your file is under 10MB.

## Which Format Should You Use?

| Format | Compatibility | Animations preserved |
|---|---|---|
| PPTX | High (PowerPoint required) | ✅ Yes |
| PDF | Universal | ❌ No (static slides) |
| Google Slides link | Requires internet | ✅ Yes |
| Keynote | Mac only | ✅ Yes |

**Best universal choice:** Export to PDF from your phone if you don't know what software the laptop has.

## How to Export to PDF From Your Phone

**PowerPoint Mobile (iOS/Android):**
Click the three dots → Export → PDF

**Google Slides (iOS/Android):**
Three dots → Share & Export → Send a Copy → PDF

**Keynote (iOS):**
Tap the three dots → Export → PDF → Send to Files

## Prepare Before You Leave for the Meeting

- Export your presentation to PDF the night before
- Save it in your Files app (easy to find quickly)
- Keep Wi-Fi or mobile data on during the presentation room setup

> ⚠️ **Disclaimer:** OneTimeDrop deletes your presentation file after 10 minutes. Download it to the laptop before the session expires. Don't upload confidential investor presentations from public shared computers.

## FAQ

**Q: What if the room's computer has no internet?**  
A: You'd need a USB drive. Bring one as backup for mission-critical presentations.

**Q: Can I edit the PPTX after transferring?**  
A: Yes — download it to the laptop, then open in PowerPoint or LibreOffice.

**Q: What if the file is over 50MB?**  
A: Compress images in your presentation, or export as PDF (which removes animations but will be smaller).
