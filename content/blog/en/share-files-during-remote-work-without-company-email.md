## Why People Reach for Email Too Fast

Remote teams often use email as a backup for everything, even when it is the wrong tool. The result is version confusion, oversized attachments, and sensitive files sitting in inboxes longer than anyone intended.

A better remote workflow uses the lightest tool that still matches the task.

## Better Options Than Company Email

### Live Handoff During a Call
If both people are actively working, a direct session-based transfer is faster than attaching files back and forth.

### Expiring Link for Follow-Up
If the recipient needs time, use a secure link with an expiry instead of forwarding to multiple inboxes.

### System of Record for Final Files
Move the approved version into your real document platform once the temporary exchange is complete.

## Why This Helps Security Too

Every forwarded email creates another copy to manage. A temporary transfer narrows the access window and reduces the chance that personal inboxes become unofficial storage.

| Method | Good for | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Company email | Formal messages | Attachment sprawl |
| Session transfer | Live exchange | Requires both sides present |
| Expiring link | Short-term async work | Needs link discipline |
| Shared drive | Final storage | Permission creep |

## Practical Rule

Use email to communicate, not to function as your default file pipeline. If the file is intermediate, time-sensitive, or large, pick a transfer workflow first and archive the final version later.

## FAQ

**Q: Is it okay to use personal email instead?**  
A: Usually no. That creates even more governance and privacy problems.

**Q: What if policy requires approved tools only?**  
A: Follow policy. The point is to avoid using email as a substitute when a better approved transfer option exists.

**Q: How do I avoid version confusion?**  
A: Keep temporary exchanges short and move the final approved file into one official location.
