## What Is a QR Code?

A QR code (Quick Response code) is the square barcode pattern you've probably spotted on restaurant menus, product packaging, and billboards. When you scan one with your phone camera, it usually opens a website automatically.

In file transfer, QR codes are used as a shortcut — instead of typing a long web address or code manually, you just scan the square with your camera and you're instantly connected.

## Why QR Codes Make File Transfer Easier

Before QR codes were common, sharing a session link meant:
1. Typing a URL on your phone keyboard
2. Finding and entering a long code
3. Or plugging in a USB cable

With a QR code, you just point your camera. One second, and you're connected.

## What You'll Need

- A computer (laptop or desktop) with any modern browser
- A smartphone (iPhone or Android) with a working camera
- Internet access on both devices

That's it. No USB cable. No app downloads. No accounts.

## Your First QR Code File Transfer: Step by Step

### Part 1: Set Up on the Computer

1. Open your browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, or Safari)
2. Type in the address bar: **onetimedrop.io** and press Enter
3. You'll see the OneTimeDrop homepage
4. A large square QR code appears on the left, and an 8-digit number code on the right
5. Leave this page open — don't close or refresh it

### Part 2: Connect Your Phone

**If your phone camera auto-scans QR:**
6. Open your camera app
7. Point the camera at the QR code on the computer screen
8. Hold still for a second — a notification or banner should appear at the top of your phone screen
9. Tap that notification — it opens a new browser page on your phone automatically

**If you'd rather type the code:**
6. On your phone, open a browser
7. Go to **onetimedrop.io/join**
8. Type the 8-digit code shown on the computer screen
9. Tap "Connect to desktop"

### Part 3: Upload Your File

10. On your phone, you'll see a blue "Select files" button — tap it
11. Choose a photo or document from your phone
12. The upload starts automatically — you'll see the file name and a progress bar
13. When it says "Done" (or you see a checkmark), the file has arrived on the computer

### Part 4: Download on the Computer

14. On the computer, your file appears in a list
15. Click the orange "Download" button next to your file
16. The file saves to your Downloads folder
17. Open it from there — done!

## What Happens to My File?

After 10 minutes, OneTimeDrop automatically deletes all files from its servers. This is a privacy feature — files don't hang around. So download what you need before the session expires.

The countdown timer is visible on the computer screen.

## Common Mistakes (And How to Fix Them)

**"The QR code didn't scan"**  
→ Make sure there's enough light. Hold your phone steadier. Move slightly closer or farther.

**"The 10 minutes ran out before I downloaded"**  
→ Refresh the computer page and start a new session. Upload and download again.

**"I can't find the file I uploaded"**  
→ On most computers, downloaded files go to the Downloads folder. On Windows: File Explorer → Downloads. On Mac: Finder → Downloads.
