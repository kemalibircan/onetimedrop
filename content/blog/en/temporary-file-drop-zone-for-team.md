## What a Temporary Drop Zone Is

A temporary file drop zone is a short-lived place where people can upload or download the files needed for one task, one meeting, or one shift. It is useful when a full shared drive would be overkill or risky.

The goal is to reduce friction and reduce retention at the same time.

## A Simple Team Workflow

1. Decide who needs upload access and who only needs download access.
2. Use a session-based handoff like [OneTimeDrop](/) when the exchange is happening live in the same room or meeting.
3. Use expiring links for remote teammates who need a little more time.
4. Move final approved files into your permanent system of record.
5. Delete the temporary handoff area after the task is complete.

## Why This Works Better Than a Shared Folder for Short Jobs

Permanent folders accumulate stale files, wrong versions, and extra people. A temporary zone keeps the exchange focused on the current task: today's print job, today's event assets, or today's signed forms.

| Approach | Best for | Good part | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Temporary drop zone | Time-bound work | Less clutter, less exposure | Requires process discipline |
| Permanent shared folder | Ongoing teamwork | History and persistence | Easy to over-share |

## Guardrails to Set

- Use clear naming rules before upload.
- Pick an expiry point before the work starts.
- Avoid mixing confidential and non-confidential files in the same handoff.
- Assign one person to move final files into long-term storage.

## FAQ

**Q: Is this only for office teams?**  
A: No. It also works for classrooms, events, studios, volunteer groups, and print counters.

**Q: Should the drop zone replace cloud storage?**  
A: No. It should reduce transfer friction before files are archived in the correct place.

**Q: What makes a drop zone secure?**  
A: Short access windows, limited scope, and deliberate cleanup.
