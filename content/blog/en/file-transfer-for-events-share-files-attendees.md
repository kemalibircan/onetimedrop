## Why Events Need a Different Workflow

Events are chaotic: people arrive late, Wi-Fi changes, and nobody wants to install an app for a single handout. The safest event file-sharing workflow is the one with the fewest steps for attendees and the fewest long-term leftovers for organizers.

That usually means QR-based access plus short-lived distribution.

## A Simple Event Setup

1. Prepare the final attendee files before the event starts.
2. Put the QR code where people naturally look: registration desk, welcome slide, booth counter, or printed sign.
3. Use [OneTimeDrop](/) or a time-limited handoff page when distribution is live and immediate.
4. Keep the file list small and clearly named.
5. Retire the transfer once the event window closes.

## What to Share This Way

- Agenda PDFs
- Speaker slides
- Promo images or press kits
- Feedback forms
- Venue maps or parking instructions

## Why This Beats Emailing Everyone

Email assumes you collected addresses, wrote the list correctly, and reached the right inbox before the moment passed. In-person file access is faster when attendees are already in front of you.

| Method | Best for | Good part | Weak part |
|---|---|---|---|
| QR handoff | Live event distribution | Immediate access | Needs clear signage |
| Email follow-up | Post-event recap | Works remotely | Slow during the event |
| Shared folder | Small groups | Persistent access | Too much clutter for public use |

## FAQ

**Q: Should attendees upload files too?**  
A: Only if the workflow is controlled. For most events, one-way distribution is cleaner.

**Q: What if the venue Wi-Fi is poor?**  
A: Test ahead of time and keep a fallback such as a staffed laptop or USB copy for presenters.

**Q: Is QR easier than a short URL?**  
A: Usually yes, especially when people are already on their phones.
