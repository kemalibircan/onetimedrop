## Printing From Your Phone: Why It's Still Annoying

Mobile printing has improved — AirPrint works great if the printer is yours. But when you're at a hotel, office, school, or copy shop and need to print from your personal phone, you're usually stuck:

- No shared Wi-Fi printer access
- Can't install apps
- Don't want to email from a public computer
- No USB cable handy

Here's the easiest solution.

## What File Format Should I Use for Printing?

Always prefer **PDF** — it preserves fonts, layout, and image quality regardless of which software the computer uses to open it.

If you have a Word doc or Google Doc on your phone:
- Google Docs: Share → Email as PDF
- Microsoft Word Mobile: tap ⋯ → Export → PDF

Photos for printing: JPEG or PNG both work, but PDF gives a predictable print size.
