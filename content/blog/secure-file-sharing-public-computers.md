## The Risks of Public Computers

Public computers present serious privacy risks:
- **Keyloggers** may record passwords you type
- **Browser history** is often not cleared between users
- **Downloaded files** may persist in the Downloads folder
- **Browser sessions** may not be properly logged out

Despite these risks, sometimes you have no choice — a library, copy shop, hotel business center, or shared work computer. Here's how to stay safe.

## ✅ The Public Computer File Transfer Checklist

### Before You Start
- [ ] Use incognito/private browsing mode (prevents history, cookies, and auto-fill)
- [ ] Check who's around you (shoulder surfing)
- [ ] Verify you're on the real website (check the full URL in the address bar)

### During the Transfer
- [ ] Transfer only what you need — don't upload extra sensitive files
- [ ] Don't log into any personal accounts (email, Google, etc.)
- [ ] Don't click "Remember me" or "Save password" on any prompts

### After Downloading
- [ ] Download your file
- [ ] Print immediately if that's the goal
- [ ] **Delete the downloaded file from the Downloads folder**
- [ ] Clear the session in OneTimeDrop (or wait 10 min for auto-expiry)
- [ ] Close all browser tabs
- [ ] Clear browser history and cookies (if possible): Settings → Clear browsing data

### If You Accidentally Logged In
- [ ] Log out of every account
- [ ] Revoke sessions from your phone (e.g., Google: Security → Devices)

## What NOT to Transfer on Public Computers

Even with a safe transfer method, avoid uploading:
- Government IDs (passport, driver's license)
- Bank documents
- Medical records
- Highly personal photos you'd be embarrassed by

> ⚠️ **Disclaimer:** OneTimeDrop files are auto-deleted from the server after 10 minutes. However, any file you download to a public computer persists locally until deleted. Always manually remove downloaded files from shared computers.

## FAQ

**Q: Is incognito mode enough to make public computers safe?**  
A: Incognito mode prevents browser history and cookie storage, but doesn't protect against keyloggers or other malware installed on the computer.

**Q: What if I need to log into Gmail to get a file?**  
A: Log in using incognito mode, download what you need, then log out immediately. After your session, revoke that session from your primary device.

**Q: Is the file transfer itself safe?**  
A: The transfer to OneTimeDrop is HTTPS encrypted. The risk isn't in the transfer — it's what happens after the file is downloaded.
