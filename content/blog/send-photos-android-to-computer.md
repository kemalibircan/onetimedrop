## Android File Transfer: Still More Painful Than It Should Be

Connecting an Android phone to a Windows PC via USB is straightforward — but to a Mac, you need to install Android File Transfer. On a computer you don't own, you can't install anything at all.

The good news: you don't need to.

## Send Android Photos With Just a Browser

[OneTimeDrop](/) handles the transfer entirely through your browser. No app download, no pairing required.

### Quick Guide

1. **On the computer**, open [onetimedrop.io](/)
2. A **QR code and session code** appear right away
3. **On your Android phone**, open your camera or QR scanner app
4. Scan the code — it opens OneTimeDrop's join page automatically
5. Tap **Select files**, pick your photos from the gallery
6. **Upload them** — progress shows on your phone
7. **On the computer**, click Download next to each photo

Photos transfer at full original quality.

## Why Android Users Love This Method

- Works on Samsung, Pixel, OnePlus, Xiaomi — any Android phone
- No need to install Android File Transfer or drivers
- Works on Mac, Windows, Linux, Chromebook
- Photos don't get compressed (unlike WhatsApp)
- Files auto-delete after 10 minutes — no data trails

## Batch Transfer: Sending Multiple Photos

On Android, most browsers support multi-file selection with the native file picker. Tap and hold images to select more than one. OneTimeDrop supports up to **20 files per session**, each up to 50MB.

For very large photo libraries, consider compressing into a ZIP file first on your phone before uploading.

## What About Nearby Share (Android's AirDrop)?

Google's Nearby Share is Android's native wireless transfer tool — but both devices need to be on the same Wi-Fi network and have it enabled. On a computer you don't control (school, library, work), Nearby Share likely won't work at all.

OneTimeDrop requires only browser access and internet — no shared Wi-Fi, no device pairing.

## Privacy & Safety Tip

> ⚠️ **Disclaimer:** OneTimeDrop files are stored for 10 minutes then deleted automatically. Don't upload personal ID documents or private photos from public computers.

Always verify you're on a real OneTimeDrop session (check the URL in the address bar) and disconnect when done.

## FAQ

**Q: Does the phone and computer need to be on the same Wi-Fi?**  
A: No. Each device just needs its own internet connection (mobile data works fine).

**Q: Does this compress my photos?**  
A: No. Files transfer at original quality and size.

**Q: How long does it take?**  
A: A typical 5MB photo takes 1–3 seconds on a normal connection.

**Q: Where does the session code come from?**  
A: The computer generates a unique 8-digit code every session. It expires after 10 minutes.
